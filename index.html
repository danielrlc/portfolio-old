About

I'm a self-taught frontend web developer. My interest in the area began when I was creating a WordPress website for learning Finnish. I started digging into the code and making tweaks, but of course I didn't really know what I was doing. After a while, I decided I wanted to learn how to do it properly.

I began by taking a three-month course with Thinkful. I liked the course as it threw me straight into it: building projects, using Github, engaging with the community, and having weekly mentor sessions. Since completing the course, I've been fleshing out my skills by taking other online courses, reading books and listening to many hours of podcasts.

I have a background in language training and live in Finland with my wife and two young daughters.

Project notes

John Williams quiz

I made this short quiz as simple and interactive as possible. Wrong answers turn red, sound an ominous gong and trigger feedback, which changes for each wrong answer. The right answer turns green and sounds a "ta-da" fanfare (which you might find a tiny bit irritating after a while).

The quiz demonstrates my improving frontend skills in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, usability and design. The site has responsive images, and the quiz content is injected via JavaScript. I created a prototype object from which all the question objects were created.

I am getting better at making colours work together and using white space effectively. I used Paletton.com to help me choose a suitable blue, green and red colour palette to work with.

Hot or cold game

The app picks a random number between 1 and 100 and you have to guess it. With each guess, you get feedback on how close you got.

For this project, I was required to add interactivity to the HTML and CSS files I was presented with. As a result, I learnt a very useful skill: how to take on a project that's already been started.
